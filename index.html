About



More than a decade ago, I began my career in technology. After years in retail, I welcomed the challenge when Blackbaud, one of the leading CRMs for nonprofit donor management, hired me for the role of Support Analyst for Raisers Edge. Three years in support expanded my product knowledge and honed my troubleshooting skills. I also gained passion for the nonprofit world.

When the opportunity arose to work for a nonprofit and have a direct impact on their mission, I accepted a position with the Indiana State Museum and Historic Sites as their Database Coordinator. I spent the next year restructuring their database, streamlining data entry procedures, and documenting best practices. The joy of working in a museum was a huge bonus.

In the spring of 2015, I was recruited by a former colleague to join Bloomerang, an online CRM startup for nonprofit donor management, as a Project Manager for the implementation department. Over the next six years, I aided in the conversion of over 500 databases, mapped data from more than 60 different CRMs, gained knowledge in MySQL, MS SQL Server, PostgreSQL, Access, and Excel and rose to Senior Data Migration Specialist.

In early 2021, I decided it was time to try a different path. I had spent years working with developers and was fascinated by their work. My curiosity and creativity lead me to Eleven Fifty Academy to study web development. Today, I am proud to say I am a web developer. My goal is to continue to use my love for art and mindful design to further develop my skills.
